ABOUT ME

Hi! My name is Mohamed Maklad and I am a third-year Computer Engineering Co-op student at McMaster University.
Born in Cairo, Egypt, I shortly thereafter moved to the United States where I grew up before subsequently moving to Canada. Ever since grade 5, I have had a passion for taking electronics apart and trying to understand how they work. I would also try to fix old electronics; though, with tiny 9-year-old hands and no training, I was admittedly not very good at it.
Through high school, I became fascinated by programming and computer science. The idea of controlling a computer by a few lines of code intrigued me. I saw the option of outsourcing any repetitive calculations or tasks to a computer as a convenient way to complete otherwise tedious tasks. This interest and curiosity led me to pursuing an academic career in Computer Engineering at an institution known for its prestigious Engineering curriculum, McMaster University. Since beginning my studies, my love for computer science has grown so much that I have chosen to not only major in Computer Engineering (Co-op) but also pursue a minor in computer science.

Only in my second year, I have already begun to work with the McMaster Solar Car Team to apply my knowledge and learn from other students. I use what I learn in class to work on the electronics of the car. I have also begun to help with building the chassis, which has facilitated a greater knowledge in mechanical engineering.

Have questions about Computer Engineering at Mac? Interested in joining Solar Car? Contact me.
